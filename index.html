    Welcome

    My name is Samuele Soraggi, and I am a postdoctoral fellow at BiRC (Bioinformatics Research Center) at the university of Aarhus. Here are some links to useful personal resources:

    My research

    During my job as a PhD fellow in Copenhagen, my research was mostly focused on the analysis of admixtures and ploidy levels from NGS data. I implemented two tools: a test for admixture between populations in R/C++ and a tool for munging NGS data to find ploidy levels in R/Rcpp.

    Currently, as a postdoc, I am using state-of-the-art methods in machine learning for the analysis of single cell sequencing data. Specifically, the interest is on the comparison between primate testis tissues, exploration of single nuclei vs single cell data, and analysis of infertile testis tissue.

    Professional interests

    I am in general interest in applying statistics to the analysis of data. This matches with a broader interest in learning programming languages, master the implementation of ad-hoc analysis tools, and extracting the essential informations to find cool things the data can tell us.
