    Home

    My name is Samuele Soraggi, and I work as a (bioinformatics) Data scientist for the Health Data Science Sandbox at Aarhus University. I focus on making and teaching online training modules, collecting non-sensitive and synthetic data, and implementing research services into a single infrastructure, with the final objective of lifting students' and researchers' possibilities in learning and researching. I was previously a supercomputing facilitator and a postdoctoral fellow in bioinformatics (at BiRC - Bioinformatics Research Center) at the University of Aarhus, and a PhD in statistics at the department of mathematical sciences of the University of Copenhagen.

    Professional experience

    During my job as a PhD fellow in Copenhagen, my research was mostly related to population genetics and bioinformatics, with the analysis of admixtures and ploidy levels from NGS data. I implemented two tools in R/C++/Rcpp to test for admixture between populations and NGS data to find ploidy levels, and wrote a general framework for graphs representing populations migrations and splits.

    As a postdoc researcher, I used machine learning and AI in interdisciplinary projects for the analysis of single cell sequencing data. The interest was on the comparison between primate testis tissues, exploration of nuclei vs cells, comparison of tumor types.

    My postdoc generated an interest in High Performance Computing (HPC), machine learning and analysis of Big Data. This lead to a position as supercomputing facilitator for the European HPC (EuroHPC) project.

    I joined in 2021 the Health Data Science Sandbox, collecting and creating data, training resources, and specialized tools for the students and researchers of the health data sciences.

    I am in general interested in applying computational statistics to the analysis of data. Of special interest are ML/AI applications to Big Data, but also deeper knowledge of the essential computational statistician toolbox. This matches with a broader interest in learning programming languages, master the implementation of ad-hoc analysis tools, and extracting the essential informations to find relevant facts the data can tell us.
